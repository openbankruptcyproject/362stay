Exceptions to the Automatic Stay

Section 362(b) lists the actions that are NOT stopped by the automatic stay. These exceptions allow certain proceedings to continue despite the bankruptcy filing.

Overview

While the automatic stay under Section 362(a) is one of the broadest injunctions in federal law, it is not absolute. Section 362(b) currently contains over 25 specific exceptions. Below are the exceptions most commonly encountered by consumer and small business debtors, grouped by category.

Criminal and Government Actions

Criminal Proceedings

Section 362(b)(1)

The commencement or continuation of a criminal action or proceeding against the debtor. Bankruptcy does not shield anyone from criminal prosecution. This includes pending criminal cases, new indictments, probation revocation, and sentencing proceedings.

Government Police and Regulatory Power

Section 362(b)(4)

Actions by a governmental unit to enforce its police or regulatory power. This includes environmental enforcement (EPA cleanup orders), building code enforcement, health and safety inspections, professional licensing proceedings, and consumer protection enforcement. The key question is whether the government is acting to protect the public interest (excepted) or to collect money (stayed).

Domestic and Family

Domestic Support Obligations

Section 362(b)(2)

Collection of domestic support obligations (child support and alimony) from property that is not property of the estate. Wage withholding for current support continues. Paternity actions, support modification actions, and divorce proceedings related to support can proceed. This is one of the most important exceptions for family law practitioners.

Divorce and Dissolution

Section 362(b)(2)(A)(ii)-(iv)

The establishment of paternity, the establishment or modification of domestic support orders, and proceedings concerning child custody or visitation are all excepted from the stay. The divorce itself can usually proceed, but division of marital property that is property of the estate may still be stayed. Courts draw a distinction between the divorce decree and the property division.

Tax-Related Exceptions

Tax Audits and Assessments

Section 362(b)(9)

An audit by a governmental unit to determine tax liability, the issuance of a notice of tax deficiency, a demand for tax returns, and the making of an assessment and issuance of a demand for payment of such assessment are all excepted. However, actual collection activity -- levies, seizures of property, garnishments -- remains stayed. The distinction is between determining how much is owed (allowed) and collecting it (stayed).

Tax Court Proceedings

Section 362(b)(9)

The commencement or continuation of a proceeding before the United States Tax Court is excepted from the stay. Debtors can still contest tax liability in Tax Court during bankruptcy.

Real Property and Eviction

Eviction with Pre-Petition Judgment

Section 362(b)(22)

If the landlord obtained a judgment for possession of the debtor's residential property before the bankruptcy petition was filed, the landlord may continue the eviction. The debtor has a narrow right to delay under Section 362(l) by filing a certification (within 30 days) that the debtor can cure the default under state law, along with a deposit of one month's rent. But this protection is limited and temporary.

Eviction Based on Endangerment or Illegal Drug Use

Section 362(b)(23)

If the eviction is based on endangerment of the property or illegal use of controlled substances on the property, the landlord may proceed regardless of the filing. The debtor cannot use the certification process under Section 362(l) to delay evictions on these grounds.

Financial and Securities

Setoff by Commodity or Securities Broker

Section 362(b)(6), (b)(7), (b)(17), (b)(27)

Various exceptions protect the ability of financial institutions, commodity brokers, forward contract merchants, swap participants, and securities clearing agencies to exercise contractual rights of setoff and to close out and net positions. These exceptions protect the stability of financial markets.

Presentment of Negotiable Instruments

Section 362(b)(11)

The presentment of a negotiable instrument and the giving of notice of and protesting dishonor of such an instrument is excepted. This allows checks to be processed and negotiable instruments to clear through the banking system.

Other Notable Exceptions

ERISA and Pension Loans

Section 362(b)(19)

Withholding of income from the debtor's wages to repay a loan from an ERISA-qualified pension, profit-sharing, or stock bonus plan is excepted from the stay, provided the loan was made before the filing.

Acts to Perfect Interests Within Statutory Grace Periods

Section 362(b)(3)

A creditor may perfect a security interest (such as recording a lien) within the time permitted under Sections 546(b) and 547(e)(2)(A). This prevents the automatic stay from creating a gap that turns properly perfected liens into avoidable preferences.

HUD-Insured Mortgage Foreclosure (Section 362(b)(8))

Section 362(b)(8)

Certain actions by the Secretary of Housing and Urban Development to foreclose a mortgage or deed of trust insured under the National Housing Act are excepted if the mortgage is held by HUD or insured under a specific HUD program.

The full text of Section 362(b) contains additional exceptions for specialized situations including student loan proceedings under certain conditions, securities regulatory actions, and more. For the complete statutory text, see 11 U.S.C. Section 362(b) on Cornell LII.
