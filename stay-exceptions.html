Automatic Stay Exceptions -- What Is Not Covered

Over 25 categories of actions that the automatic stay does not stop

Section 362(b) Exceptions

While the automatic stay is broad, it is not absolute. Section 362(b) of the Bankruptcy Code lists specific actions that are not stopped by the filing of a bankruptcy petition. Here are the most important ones:

Criminal Proceedings

The automatic stay does not stop criminal prosecutions. If you are facing criminal charges -- even for writing bad checks or fraud -- the criminal case proceeds regardless of your bankruptcy filing. The stay only applies to civil collection activity, not criminal law enforcement.

Domestic Support Obligations

Child support and alimony (domestic support obligations under Section 101(14A)) are not stayed. Actions to establish paternity, establish or modify a support order, collect support from property that is not part of the bankruptcy estate, and withhold income for support all continue unaffected.

This is one of the broadest exceptions. Even wage garnishment for child support or alimony continues through bankruptcy.

Tax Proceedings

Several tax-related actions are exempt from the stay:

  • Tax audits and assessments
  • Issuance of a notice of tax deficiency
  • Demand for tax returns
  • Making an assessment of any tax and issuing a notice and demand for payment

However, the IRS and state tax agencies cannot collect on tax debts during the stay (unless they get relief from stay). The exception allows auditing and assessment, but not collection.

Eviction Actions (Limited)

Under Section 362(b)(22), if the landlord obtained a judgment for possession of the property before the bankruptcy was filed, the eviction can proceed. The debtor can delay this by filing a certification and depositing rent with the court, but the window is narrow (30 days).

If no pre-petition eviction judgment exists, the stay does stop the eviction temporarily -- though the landlord can quickly file for relief from stay.

Regulatory and Police Power Actions

Government actions to enforce regulatory or police powers are not stayed. This includes environmental enforcement, health and safety regulation, licensing actions, and similar governmental exercises of regulatory authority. The test is whether the government is acting in its regulatory capacity (not stayed) or as a creditor seeking to collect money (stayed).

Other Notable Exceptions

  • Pension loan setoff: Loans from retirement plans can continue to be repaid through payroll deduction
  • Presentment of negotiable instruments: Checks can still be deposited and processed
  • Securities regulation: SEC and CFTC enforcement actions
  • Certain setoffs: Setoffs by commodity brokers, forward contract merchants, and financial institutions under safe harbors
